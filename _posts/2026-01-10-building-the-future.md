Walk through the renovated tech districts of Ljubljana or Zagreb today and you will find something that would have seemed improbable a decade ago: coworking spaces filled with young developers building software for clients across Europe.

The region's emergence as a modest but growing technology hub owes much to circumstance. Salaries that seem modest by Western European standards go considerably further in the Western Balkans. English proficiency among the younger generation is high. And a diaspora scattered across the continent has begun trickling back, bringing skills and connections acquired abroad.

## A new generation of founders

The startup ecosystem has matured considerably since the early 2010s. Accelerators, venture capital, and government incentives have created an environment where entrepreneurship is not just possible but increasingly attractive.

"I worked in Munich for eight years," says one 34-year-old developer who returned to Ljubljana in 2023. "I can do the same work here for the same clients, but my quality of life is incomparably better."
